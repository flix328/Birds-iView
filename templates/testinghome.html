Birds iView is a better way to undertake bird counts for long-term bird monitoring.
The idea is to fly drones over bird populations and take aerial images.
These images can then be sifted through later to count what birds there are.

How to Use the "Plan" Page

Find where you want to do your bird count. It might be easier to find the general location in the Streets mode and then switch to Satellite mode to select the survey area precisely.

Select the area you want to survey. Click on the map to create a boundary points, drag them around and click on them to remove them. You can also click the "Clear" button to remove everything from the map.
Note: Each new boundary point will be inserted into the closest edge of the polygon.

Adjust the drone altitude. Click on the altitude icon and then move the slider up and down. Flying low means good quality images but causes long flight paths and possible disturbance to the birds below. Flying high means short flight paths but worse image quality.
To hide the slider, click the altitude icon again or press "esc" on your keyboard.

Adjust the heading. Flying parallel or perpendicular to the long axis of the survey area usually produces good short flight paths.

Adjust the overlap between images. Large overlap reduces the changes of missing details between images, but also makes longer flight paths.

Enter the settings of the drone camera. Larger view angle makes shorter flight paths but worsens image quality, and higher resolution improves image quality.

Type the names of bird species that you're expecting to find in the survey, and add them to the list by pressing Enter when they appear in the autofill. Check that they all appear green. If they are orange or red, they are too small to detect them in the result images, so by flying lower or by using a better camera.
You can remove species by double clicking on them.

When you're happy with the generated flight path, click the Export button to export it. This will download a csv file which is specifically formatted to be imported into the Litchi Mission Hub.

Import the csv file into the Litchi Mission Hub. From there you are ready to download the flight path onto your phone and do the bird count at the site.
The Litchi app costs roughly $40
